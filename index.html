Welcome

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Geography at the University of Delaware (expected May 2026), working on global irrigation, water scarcity, and food sustainability in the 21st century.

My work sits at the intersection of the Water–Food–Energy Nexus, combining environmental datasets, geospatial analytics, and modeling to inform sustainable and resilient food–water systems.

About

I am an environmental scientist focused on sustainable water and food systems. I develop and leverage large-scale environmental datasets and models to quantify risks, trade-offs, and opportunities for climate-resilient agriculture.
