Welcome

Hi, I’m Endalkachew (Endi) Kebede—an interdisciplinary hydrologist and geospatial data scientist working at the intersection of water, agriculture, and global food systems. My research examines how irrigation dynamics, climate variability, and land-use change reshape freshwater availability and food-system sustainability across local to global scales. My work integrates hydrological modeling, remote sensing, machine learning, and open geospatial data to diagnose water scarcity risks and identify policy-relevant adaptation pathways. A central contribution of my research is the development of MIRCA-OS, a global open-source dataset of monthly irrigated and rainfed cropland areas for the 21st century. I also contribute to international efforts advancing open agricultural and water datasets, including HarvestStat and next-generation global irrigation mapping initiatives. This work enables fine-scale assessment of the evolution of food production and agricultural water use in the 21st century. My academic background spans engineering, science, and geospatial disciplines, with training experiences across Africa, Asia, Europe, and the United States. Before my PhD, I worked as a lecturer, researcher, and engineer on applied water and agricultural sustainability challenges in Ethiopia. Beyond research, I am committed to teaching, mentoring, and open science, and I enjoy building inclusive research communities that connect data, people, and real-world decision-making.
